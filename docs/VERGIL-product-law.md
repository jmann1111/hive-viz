# Vergil Product Law

This document records the product-law direction for the archive intelligence UX.
It is intentionally narrower than an architecture rewrite.
It exists so future merge and integration decisions do not mistake the current scaffold for the final product.

## Current Truth

- The current bottom orb panel is a temporary retrieval scaffold.
- It is not the final product UX.
- Keep the current retrieval stack and safety logic.

## Product Law

- This is Vergil, not a generic orb.
- Vergil is a diegetic in-world archive intelligence.
- Vergil should exist spatially in the galaxy, not only as flat UI chrome.
- Retrieval should eventually feel embodied and guided.
- When a note is resolved, Vergil should lead motion through the graph alongside the camera and arrive at the destination.
- The target feel is closer to Guilty Spark or a hovering guide presence than a plain search box.

## Guardrail For Future Work

- Do not mistake the current bottom-panel retrieval scaffold for the final UX direction.
- Future integration should preserve the current retrieval safety and resolution rules while moving the presentation toward Vergil's in-world presence.
- If a merge decision conflicts with this law, treat this document as the product-direction tie breaker unless Jason explicitly overrides it.
